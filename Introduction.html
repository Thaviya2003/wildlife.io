Sri Lanka, formerly known as Ceylon, is an island nation located in South Asia in the Indian Ocean. It is situated southeast of India and is separated from the Indian subcontinent by the Palk Strait. Colombo, the largest city, serves as the commercial capital, while Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte is the legislative capital. Sri Lanka has a rich history dating back over 3,000 years, with influences from various cultures, including Indian, Arab, and European. The island is known for its diverse landscapes, ranging from lush tea plantations and tropical beaches to ancient cities and dense forests. The country is characterized by a multi-ethnic and multi-religious population, with Sinhala being the major ethnic group and Buddhism the predominant religion. Tamil, Moor, and other communities also contribute to the cultural diversity. Sri Lanka has faced challenges such as a prolonged civil conflict that ended in 2009 and natural disasters like tsunamis and floods. Despite these challenges, the nation has made progress in economic development, particularly in sectors like tourism, agriculture, and services. The island is famous for its tea production, gemstones, vibrant festivals, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites such as Sigiriya and Galle. Sri Lanka's wildlife, including elephants and leopards, attracts nature enthusiasts, while its cuisine, influenced by various cultures, offers a delightful culinary experience.
